Essential Apps for China Travel: A Foreigner's Complete Guide 2026

Published: May 2026 | Reading time: 10 minutes

Traveling in China without the right apps is like navigating without a map. China's digital ecosystem operates differently from the rest of the world, with its own super-apps that handle everything from payments to transportation. This guide reveals the must-have Chinese apps that will make your trip seamless, efficient, and enjoyable.

The Reality of China's Digital Landscape

Google, Facebook, WhatsApp, Uber, and many Western apps don't work in China. Instead, China has developed its own comprehensive ecosystem of super-apps. The good news? These apps are incredibly sophisticated and often more convenient than their Western counterparts. Install and set them up before arrival, and you'll navigate China like a local.

The Essential Apps: Your Digital Toolkit

WeChat

The Everything App | Communication + Payment + Services

WeChat isn't just a messaging app—it's China's digital Swiss Army knife. Used by over 1.3 billion people, it handles messaging, payments, taxi booking, food delivery, hotel reservations, and even government services.

Messaging WeChat Pay Mini Programs QR Code Scanner

Pro tip: Set up WeChat and verify your account before arriving in China. Foreign credit cards can now be linked to WeChat Pay, making transactions seamless.

Alipay

Payment Powerhouse | Shopping + Finance + Lifestyle

Alipay is WeChat Pay's main competitor and equally essential. Many vendors prefer one over the other, so having both ensures you're never stuck without payment options. Alipay also offers translation features and tourist-friendly services.

Mobile Payments Tourist Pass Translation Tool International Cards

Pro tip: Download the "Alipay Tour Pass" version specifically designed for international visitors. It allows you to preload money and use the app without a Chinese bank account.

DiDi

Ride-Hailing | China's Uber Alternative

DiDi dominates China's ride-hailing market. The app offers English interface options and accepts international credit cards. It's often more affordable and reliable than traditional taxis, with transparent pricing and route tracking.

Taxi & Private Cars English Interface Price Estimates Multiple Payment Options

Pro tip: Download and set up DiDi before arrival. Verification may require a phone number, so arrange your Chinese SIM or ensure international roaming works.

Meituan

Food Delivery + Services | The Ultimate Convenience App

Meituan is China's largest food delivery platform, but it also offers hotel bookings, movie tickets, bike rentals, and local services. Even if you don't order food, it's invaluable for finding restaurants, reading reviews, and exploring local options.

Food Delivery Restaurant Reviews Hotel Booking Bike Sharing

Pro tip: Use Meituan to check restaurant menus and reviews even if you dine in. The ratings and photos help you make informed choices.

高德地图 (Amap)

Navigation | Superior Maps for China

Google Maps doesn't work properly in China. Amap (Gaode Map) is the most accurate navigation app for the country, with real-time traffic updates, public transit directions, and walking routes. It integrates seamlessly with DiDi for ride-hailing.

Accurate Maps Public Transit Real-time Traffic DiDi Integration

Pro tip: Download offline maps for cities you'll visit. While the app interface is Chinese-only, the map interface is intuitive and visual.

百度地图 (Baidu Maps)

Alternative Navigation | Google Maps Replacement

Baidu Maps is another excellent navigation option with some English support. It excels at public transportation directions and offers street view functionality in major cities. Having both Amap and Baidu provides backup options.

English Support Transit Directions Street View Offline Maps

小红书 (Xiaohongshu/RED)

Lifestyle Discovery | China's Instagram + Yelp

Xiaohongshu combines social media, reviews, and recommendations. It's where Chinese users share restaurant finds, travel tips, shopping guides, and lifestyle inspiration. Use it to discover trending spots and authentic local experiences.

Restaurant Reviews Travel Guides Shopping Tips Visual Discovery

Pro tip: Use image translation to browse Chinese content. The visual nature of the app makes it easy to understand recommendations even without reading Chinese.

Pre-Arrival Setup Guide

Setting up these apps before landing in China saves significant hassle. Here's the optimal order:

1

Download VPN (if needed) — If you want continued access to Western apps and websites, install a reliable VPN before arrival. Many VPN websites are blocked in China.

2

Install WeChat — Download from your app store, register with your phone number, and complete verification. Link an international credit card if possible.

3

Set up Alipay Tour Pass — Download Alipay, select the international/tourist version, and preload funds using your foreign credit card.

4

Download Navigation Apps — Install Amap and Baidu Maps. Download offline maps for your destination cities while you have good internet.

5

Set up DiDi — Register and add payment methods. Test the interface to familiarize yourself before needing it urgently.

Payment Setup Timeline: Linking international cards to WeChat and Alipay has become easier in 2026, but verification can still take 24-48 hours. Complete this setup at least 3 days before your trip to avoid issues.

Apps That Don't Work in China

Knowing what doesn't work is equally important. These popular Western apps are blocked or severely limited:

  • Google services: Google Maps, Gmail, Google Search, YouTube
  • Social media: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, WhatsApp, Snapchat
  • Communication: Telegram, Signal, Line (limited)
  • Navigation: Google Maps (inaccurate), Waze, Apple Maps (incomplete)
  • Streaming: Netflix, Spotify, HBO Max
  • News: BBC, New York Times, most Western news sites

Alternative Solutions

Translation Apps

Google Translate works but requires VPN. Better alternatives include:

  • Pleco: The gold standard for Chinese dictionary and translation
  • Baidu Translate: Works without VPN, offers camera translation
  • Microsoft Translator: Reliable offline capability

Browser Options

  • Safari (iOS): Works normally, can access most international sites
  • Chrome: Requires VPN for full functionality
  • UC Browser / QQ Browser: Popular Chinese alternatives

Technical Tips for App Success

Phone Compatibility:
  • iPhones work perfectly; all Chinese apps support iOS
  • Most Android phones work, but Google-dependent features may have issues
  • Ensure your phone supports Chinese characters display
  • Enable international roaming or get a Chinese SIM card

Storage Management

Chinese apps tend to be large and cache-heavy:

  • Ensure at least 5GB free space for smooth operation
  • Regularly clear cache in app settings
  • WeChat especially tends to accumulate storage quickly

The Bottom Line

China's app ecosystem, while initially intimidating, offers remarkable convenience once mastered. The super-app concept—where single platforms handle multiple services—actually simplifies travel when understood.

With WeChat and Alipay for payments, DiDi for transport, Amap for navigation, and Meituan for food discovery, you're equipped for virtually any situation. Set these up before arrival, spend an hour familiarizing yourself with their interfaces, and you'll navigate China with confidence.
