The Entry Process: What to Expect

Modern China's entry procedures have been streamlined significantly. Here's the step-by-step experience at major international airports like Beijing Capital, Shanghai Pudong, and Guangzhou Baiyun:

1

Before You Fly

Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned stay. Book your return or onward ticket—you'll need to show proof of departure within 30 days.

2

Arrival Card

Complete the arrival card during your flight or at electronic kiosks. Many airports now offer digital completion via QR codes, available in multiple languages.

3

Immigration

Join the "Foreign Passport" queues. Have your passport, return ticket, and accommodation details ready. processing typically takes 5-10 minutes per person.

4

Customs Declaration

Declare any items over duty-free allowances. Most tourist items are duty-free, but electronics and luxury goods have limits.

Where Can You Go?

The 30-day visa-free stay allows travel throughout mainland China. You're not restricted to specific regions or cities. Popular destinations accessible under this policy include:

  • Beijing — The Great Wall, Forbidden City, Temple of Heaven
  • Shanghai — The Bund, Yu Garden, modern skyline
  • Xi'an — Terracotta Warriors, ancient city walls
  • Chengdu — Giant pandas, Sichuan cuisine
  • Guilin — Karst mountains, Li River cruises
  • Hangzhou — West Lake, tea plantations
  • Suzhou — Classical gardens, silk production
  • Guangzhou — Cantonese food, modern architecture

What You Need to Prepare

Essential Documents:
  • Passport valid for 6+ months
  • Confirmed return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel bookings)
  • Travel insurance (strongly recommended)
  • Sufficient funds for your stay

Common Questions Answered

Can I extend my 30-day stay?

Extensions are possible but require visiting local immigration offices and are not guaranteed. It's better to plan your itinerary within the 30-day window.

Can I work or study on a visa-free entry?

No. The visa-free policy is strictly for tourism and business meetings. Any employment or formal study requires the appropriate visa.

What if I overstay accidentally?

Overstaying can result in fines and potential entry bans. If you realize you'll overstay, contact local immigration immediately—proactive communication typically results in more lenient outcomes.

Does the 30 days include arrival and departure days?

Yes, the 30-day count includes both your entry and exit dates. Plan accordingly to avoid any last-minute rushes.

Maximizing Your 30 Days

With a full month available, you can experience China in depth rather than just scratching the surface. Consider these itinerary approaches:

  • Classic Route (7-10 days): Beijing → Xi'an → Shanghai
  • Southern Explorer (10-14 days): Shanghai → Hangzhou → Guilin → Guangzhou
  • Deep Dive (3-4 weeks): Combine multiple regions with slower-paced exploration
